Effects of Global Warming

  • Rising Sea Levels: Melting polar ice caps and glaciers contribute to rising sea levels, leading to coastal flooding.
  • Extreme Weather Events: More frequent and severe hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves are a direct result of climate change.
  • Loss of Biodiversity: Many species are unable to adapt to rapidly changing climates, leading to extinction or displacement.
  • Ocean Acidification: Increased CO2 in the atmosphere leads to higher CO2 levels in the oceans, affecting marine life and ecosystems.
